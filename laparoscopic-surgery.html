Autonomous Laparoscopic Surgery Simulator

Reinforcement Learning Digital Twin

Overview

Developed a digital twin simulator for autonomous laparoscopic surgery using Reinforcement Learning at Monash Deepneuron. This project creates a foundational platform for next-generation surgical AI research, enabling researchers to train and test autonomous surgical systems in a safe, controlled environment.

Significance

This simulator represents cutting-edge work in medical AI and surgical robotics. By leveraging Reinforcement Learning, the system can train AI agents to perform complex laparoscopic procedures autonomously, with potential applications in:

  • Medical training and education
  • Surgeon skill assessment and improvement
  • Development of autonomous surgical robots
  • Research into AI-assisted surgery

Technical Approach

The project implements a sophisticated RL-based system that:

  • Models realistic surgical environments as a digital twin
  • Uses Reinforcement Learning algorithms to train autonomous agents
  • Simulates complex laparoscopic procedures with high fidelity
  • Provides a safe environment for AI training without risk to patients
  • Enables reproducible research conditions for surgical AI development

Impact

  • Creates foundational platform for next-generation surgical AI research
  • Enables safe testing and training of autonomous surgical systems
  • Contributes to advancing the field of medical robotics and AI
  • Supports academic research at Monash University

Technologies

Stack: Python, Reinforcement Learning, Digital Twin Technology, AI/ML Research, PyTorch/TensorFlow
